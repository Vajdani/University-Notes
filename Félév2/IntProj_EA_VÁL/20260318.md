prompt engineering and ai use

ai is a prediction algorithm, it predicts what answer is the most likely to be correct based on the data it's been trained on 
ai models work on tokens, which are units of text or some other output

during training they analyse patterns in the given text/data

self-supervised learning: no human intervention

backpropagation: the model has billions of parameters, these are adjusted after every mistake

computers operate on numbers → words are converted to sets of numbers(embedding) 
words are points in a multidimensional space, related words are closer together

"king - man + woman = queen"

context is everything, words can mean more than one thing

self attention: the model considers the entire sentence at once

reinforcement learning: humans give feedback on the accuracy of the response 
the responses train a reward model 
the model tries to maximise the reward per response

6 pillar framework for good prompts: 
- role
- task
- format
- audience
- context
- specifics

"as an expert" - too generic 
"as a university professor of computer science" - better 
"yap" - excellent

basically just try to be specific, too little information will result in bad answers

- demography

one-shot prompting: simple prompt for a simple task 
few-shot prompting: prompt the model with a few examples before telling it the task 
chain-of-thought prompting: the model thinks step by step 
iterative prompting: revise the prompt given the quality of the output
